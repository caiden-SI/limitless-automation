## **Current End to End Production Process** 

1. **Content Pillar selected**  
   1. A decision is made which content pillar to follow based on the students custom trained Claude project   
   2. The training data is derived from a student intake form regarding branding, positioning, story, UVP, etc.  
2. **Concept creation and titling**   
   1. Based on the recommended content pillar, a script is produced by Claude   
   2. The script is revised by a human until satisfactory based on judgement   
   3. For internal project management purposes, the concept is given a 1-4 word title descriptor based on the content of the script  
   4. The title is inserted as the title of the ClickUp task in the next step  
3. **Clickup Task generation \[Manual\]**  
   1. A ClickUp task is generated with the following fields:  
      1. Title  
      2. Assignee  
      3. Script  
      4. Creative direction  
      5. Status  
4. **Dropbox Folder creation \[Manual\]**  
   1. A new folder is created in Dropbox, with a name identical to the concept title   
   2. Subfolders \[FOOTAGE\] and \[PROJECT\] are created   
5. **Shoot plan \[Manual, sometimes Automated\]**  
   1. Based on the script, a creative direction is paired next to each script line describing what should happen   
6. **Clickup Task status change \[Manual\]**  
   1. Once approved, the Clickup task status changes from \[IDEA\] to \[READY FOR SHOOTING\]  
7. **Filming \[Manual\]**  
   1. The videographer films the concept on-site at an Alpha School  
8. **Dropbox upload \[Manual\]**  
   1. The footage is uploaded to the concept specific Dropbox folder   
9. **Dropbox sync \[Automated\]**  
   1. Once uploaded, the footage syncs automatically to all team members local storage via Dropbox desktop app  
10. **Clickup Task status change \[Manual\]**  
    1. The status is changed from \[READY FOR SHOOTING\] to \[READY FOR EDITING\]  
11. **Editor assigned \[Manual\]**  
    1. An editor is manually assigned based on who has availability  
12. **New Premiere Project created \[Manual\]**  
    1. A new Premiere Pro project file is created with a file path leading to the Dropbox folder for the concept  
13. **Footage ingestion \[Manual\]**  
    1. Footage from that Dropbox folder is imported into Premiere Pro for editing   
14. **Timeline creation \[Manual\]**  
    1. A timeline within the Premiere Pro project is created   
    2. All clips are dragged into the timeline   
15. **Base editing \[Manual\]**  
    1. The editor watches through all of the footage to  
       1. Select the best clips   
       2. Organize them into/verify chronological order  
       3. Cut out pauses, double takes, peaked audio, and filler   
16. **Colorgrading \[Manual\]**  
    1. The base narrative edit is then transformed from SLog3 to Rec.709   
    2. A general “look” LUT is put on all clips   
    3. Each individual clip is adjusted for exposure and color temperature  
17. **Auto-Transcription \[Manual, by editor, but is an “automatic” feature\]**  
    1. The video is transcribed in Premiere Pro by selecting the Text panel \> Transcribe sequence   
    2. Captions are auto generated by selecting Create Captions   
       1. Depending on style, captions may be created in a few different ways with varying line length, duration, sizing, etc.   
18. **Caption QA1 \[Manual\]**  
    1. The first round of captions are reviewed for quality control by the editor for spelling, timing, and punctuation   
19. **Audio Processing \[Manual\]**  
    1. The editor manually looks at levels to ensure they are in range   
    2. The editor may opt to process audio through Adobe Podcast or the internal Premiere tool   
       1. If processed by Adobe Podcast, the video is exported as .wav \> uploaded to Adobe Podcast \> Adjusted for background and vocal processing \> downloaded \> Re-ingested into the Premiere Pro project   
20. **Creative Editing \[Manual\]**  
    1. The editor now uses a combination of creative judgement and the creative direction to add any of the following to the video  
       1. Sound effects and music from [Artlist.io](http://Artlist.io) or YouTube  
       2. Transitions and Overlays  
       3. Stock footage from [Artgrid.io](http://Artgrid.io)  
       4. Motion adjustment (Scales, transforms, masks)  
21. **Export \[Manual\]**  
    1. The video is exported from Premiere Pro   
22. **Upload \[Manual\]**  
    1. The video is uploaded to [Frame.io](http://Frame.io) for review   
23. **ClickUp Status change \[Manual\]**  
    1. The editor changes the status from \[IN EDITING\] to \[EDITED\]  
    2. The internal Frame link is pasted into the ClickUp card in the custom link field for easy access  
24. **Video Review \[Manual\]**  
    1. I review the video and leave feedback comments   
25. **ClickUp Status change \[Manual\]**  
    1. I change the status of the ClickUp card to \[NEEDS REVISIONS\]  
26. **Approval ClickUp Status change \[Manual\]**  
    1. Once approved, the ClickUp status is changed to \[DONE\]  
27. **Frame share link created \[Manual\]**  
    1. A share link is created in Frame in preparation for sending to client   
28. **Sent to client \[Manual\]**  
    1. The Frame link is sent to client for review and approval   
    2. As of today, we haven’t received a single revision from a “client” (Student) so far

## **Objective Overview**

* Automate as much of the management part of my position as possible so my time can be spent on visiting campuses and training our staff without hiring a project manager  
* Increase editor throughput by 50%-100% by eliminating non-creative, repeated, manual tasks associated with post production   
* Increase the frequency of high-performance posts using data-backed metrics publicly visible on social platforms 

## **Pre-Production Agents \+ Automations**

### **Performance Pattern Analyzer**

**Description:** Pulls data from the Content Performance Tracker, ingests view counts and growth trends, stores video transcripts, and uses pattern recognition to identify what hooks, pacing, topics, and formats are driving the most views. Outputs "make more of this" signals before concepting begins.

**Objective:** Create a closed feedback loop between published performance data and pre-production decisions — so concepting is driven by what's actually working, not intuition.

**Roadblocks:** 

* Requires consistent post URLs to be logged in the performance tracker (currently not systematic)  
* Needs a transcript storage layer built  
* Pattern recognition only becomes meaningful at \~50+ videos  
* Limited signal early on  
* Needs a defined output format the team actually checks

### **Automated Student Concepting & Scripting**

**Description:** Student inputs their current project or topic. AI generates a concept title, hook angle, and a 30–60 second script structured around what's performing best from the Pattern Analyzer. Closes the loop between data and creation.

**Objective:** Reduce time from "I want to make a video" to "I have a script" — and make every script informed by performance data rather than a blank page.

**Roadblocks:**

* Requires Performance Pattern Analyzer to be live first (dependency)  
* Needs a simple student-facing input UI (a form or ClickUp field — not a raw AI prompt)  
* Brand voice and Alpha tone needs to be embedded in the prompt layer  
* Student buy-in required — they have to actually use it

## **Post-Production Agents \+ Automations**

### **AI Transcript & Rough Cut Assembly**

**Description:** Raw footage is uploaded, AI transcribes it, identifies best takes, and assembles a rough cut timeline with filler/silence trimmed. Editor receives a structured starting point instead of raw footage.

**Objective:** Cut editor setup time by 40–60% per video — the most labor-intensive part of the edit happens before a human touches the timeline.

**Roadblocks:** 

* Needs a consistent footage upload location (Dropbox or Frame.io) with a defined folder structure  
* AI output needs to export in a format compatible with Premiere or DaVinci (XML/EDL)  
* Audio quality of raw student footage varies significantly  
* low quality inputs will produce poor cuts  
* Requires testing and calibration before production use

### **Stutter & Filler Word Detection**

**Description** AI flags every "um," "uh," stutter, and false start with exact timecodes. Editor cuts in bulk rather than scrubbing the timeline manually. Directly enforces the objective audio quality standard.

**Objective** Enforce the sub-5% audio error rate at scale without adding manual review time per video.

**Roadblocks**

* Accuracy degrades with background noise or heavy accents  
* needs a human override process for edge cases  
* Still requires editor to make final cut calls (not fully hands-off)  
* Needs to integrate into the same pipeline as the Transcript & Rough Cut tool to avoid double-processing

### **Audio Level Normalization**

**Description** Automated loudness pass (LUFS targeting) on every export. Consistent audio mastering across all deliverables without a manual step each time.

**Objective** Guarantee the "levels always mastered" quality standard is met on every single delivery — not just when an editor remembers to check.

**Roadblocks**

* LUFS target needs to be defined and agreed on per platform (TikTok, YouTube, Instagram all differ slightly)  
* Requires a post-export processing step  
* FFmpeg script or Premiere export preset  
* Needs to be embedded in the export workflow so it can't be skipped

### **Caption Spell-Check & Punctuation Automation**

**Description** AI reads all caption text post-export, flags spelling errors, brand name misspellings, missing punctuation, and formatting inconsistencies before delivery. Replaces the manual triple-check step.

**Objective** Eliminate caption errors from delivery without relying on manual review — protecting the 95% error-free rate as volume scales.

**Roadblocks**

* Requires captions to be exported as a readable file (SRT or ASS)  
* not baked into the video  
* Needs a maintained brand name dictionary (Alpha, Superbuilders, Timeback, etc.) — Punctuation rules need to be explicitly defined upfront  
* Must be integrated as a gate before the video moves to the delivery stage in ClickUp

### **Music Recommendation Automation**

**Description** Based on video brief (mood, topic, platform, pacing), AI suggests 3–5 royalty-free tracks from an approved library. Removes editor guesswork and keeps music consistent with the brief.

**Objective**: Standardize music selection across editors and reduce per-video decision time — while keeping track choices tied to the creative intent of the brief.

**Roadblocks**

* Requires an approved music library to be established and tagged with mood/energy/platform metadata first  
* Brief fields (mood, platform, pacing) need to be consistently filled in at intake  
* currently not enforced  
* Licensing needs to be confirmed for all library tracks before automation references them

**NOTE:** The clearest dependency chain — Ticketing intake fields → Music Recommendation and Concepting & Scripting → Transcript Assembly → Stutter Detection → Normalization → Caption QA. Each tool feeds the next, so build order matters.
